Gap-K%: Measuring Top-1 Prediction Gap for Detecting Pretraining Data

Minseo Kwak, Jaehyung Kim
Yonsei University

Abstract

We study pretraining data detection for large language models and propose Gap-K%, a simple and effective reference-free scoring method.

Gap-K% measures the log-probability gap between the model’s top-1 prediction and the observed token, motivated by next-token optimization dynamics, and applies local smoothing to capture sequential correlations.

Across WikiMIA and MIMIR, Gap-K% consistently outperforms strong likelihood-based baselines.

Method

Gap-K% scores a sequence by measuring how much the observed token deviates from the model’s top-1 prediction, motivated by next-token optimization dynamics.

  • Top-1 gap score: compute the (normalized) log-probability gap between the top-1 token and the target token.
  • Sequential smoothing: apply a sliding window average to reduce token-level noise and capture local correlations.
  • Bottom-k% aggregation: average the bottom k% smoothed scores to obtain a robust membership score.

Results

  • WikiMIA: Gap-K% consistently outperforms strong likelihood-based baselines across models and input lengths.
  • MIMIR: Gap-K% achieves the strongest average performance across several Pythia model sizes.

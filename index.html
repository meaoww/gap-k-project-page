Gap-K%: Measuring Top-1 Prediction Gap for Detecting Pretraining Data

Minseo Kwak, Jaehyung Kim
Yonsei University
Paper Code

Gap-K% detects pretraining data by measuring the top-1 prediction gap induced by next-token optimization dynamics, and aggregating locally smoothed gaps across sequences.

Abstract

The opacity of massive pretraining corpora in Large Language Models (LLMs) raises privacy and copyright concerns, making pretraining data detection a critical challenge. Existing methods mostly rely on token likelihoods, but often overlook divergence from the model’s top-1 prediction and local correlations between adjacent tokens.

We propose Gap-K%, a method grounded in the optimization dynamics of next-token prediction: discrepancies between the top-1 predicted token and the target token induce strong gradient signals and are explicitly penalized during training. Gap-K% leverages the log-probability gap between the top-1 token and the target, and uses a sliding window to capture local correlations and reduce token-level fluctuations.

Experiments on WikiMIA and MIMIR show that Gap-K% consistently outperforms strong baselines.

As a result, training dynamics explicitly push the model to reduce the gap between the target token and the top-1 prediction on training examples.

This observation motivates a membership signal that goes beyond token likelihood. Even when the target token has reasonably high probability, a confident top-1 misprediction (i.e., another token is preferred) induces a much stronger optimization signal than a flat or uncertain distribution.

Therefore, the log-probability gap between the target token and the top-1 token serves as a principled indicator of whether a sequence conforms to the model’s training dynamics.

Gap-K% operationalizes this insight by directly measuring and aggregating these top-1 gaps across the sequence.

Method

Gap-K% scores a sequence by measuring how much the target token deviates from the model’s top-1 prediction, then aggregating locally smoothed gaps across the sequence.

  • Top-1 gap score: for each position \(t\), let \(y_t^\*=\arg\max_{y} p(y \mid x_{\lt t})\) be the top-1 token. We compute the normalized top-1 gap: $$ g_t = \frac{\log p(x_t \mid x_{\lt t}) - \log p(y_t^\* \mid x_{\lt t})} {\mathrm{Std}\!\left(\log p(\cdot \mid x_{\lt t})\right)} \le 0. $$
  • Sequential smoothing: apply a sliding-window average over \(g_t\) to reduce token-level fluctuations and capture local correlation.
  • Bottom-K% aggregation: average the bottom \(K\%\) of smoothed scores to focus on the hardest (most indicative) segments.

Tip: we use \(\lt\) instead of < inside math to avoid HTML parsing issues.

Results

Across two benchmarks, Gap-K% demonstrates consistent improvements over strong likelihood-based baselines. On WikiMIA, Gap-K% raises AUROC relative to Min-K%++ across model sizes and input settings, and on MIMIR, it achieves the best average membership inference performance across Pythia variants.
